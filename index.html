A fashion expo is a trade show where fashion designers, manufacturers, and retailers showcase their latest collections, trends, and products. These events provide a platform for businesses to connect, network, and find new partnerships. They are a key part of the fashion industry, driving innovation and business growth. A fashion show is an event put on by a fashion designer to showcase their upcoming line of clothing and/or accessories during a fashion week. Fashion shows debut every season, particularly the spring/summer and fall/winter seasons. This is where designers seek to promote their new fashions. A fashion expos, much like a trade show, is a venue where suppliers present their newest collections, setting the tone for the upcoming season. On the other side, buyers attend to gain an insightful overview of what's next in the world of fashion.

Famous fashion designers

MANISH MALHOTRA:

The name Manish Malhotra has become almost synonymous with fashion in India. The desire of every Indian bride is to wear a lehenga designed by Manish Malhotra. After breaking into the Indian fashion industry for the first time in 1998, he began his career as a costume designer and eventually made his way up to become the most well-known designer. Manish Malhotra is the famous fashion designers in India. Both in real life and on the silver screen, he has outfitted famous people in his designs. His designs aren't only limited to the fashion scene in India; he's worked with, Kylie Minogue, Renee Witherspoon, and the late Michael Jackson, in addition to a large number of other Hollywood celebrities.

RITU KUMAR:

Designer Ritu Kumar has a deep knowledge of art, history, and musicology in addition to her creative expertise. She has given new life to the Indian heritage of handicrafts and made gorgeous garments using the technique. She has been an important figure in bridging the gap between modernism and traditionalism in recent years. In addition, she was presented with the Padma Shri award in 2013 for her significant contributions to the field of Indian fashion.
